# DES240 6: Design Project

## Project Overview

For this project, I will explore how behavioural data can be transformed into emotional and atmospheric experiences rather than being presented through traditional charts or numerical visualisations.

The project is situated within a speculative near-future scenario where digital systems continuously collect behavioural information and construct interpretations of users without directly asking for personal input. Instead of treating data as objective facts, the project investigates how systems translate behavioural patterns into emotional assumptions, identity profiles, and personalised experiences.

The working title for the project is **ECHO//PROFILE**.

The project asks:

> How might we visualise the ways digital systems interpret and construct personal identity through behavioural data?

The final outcome will be a screen-based interactive visualisation where users explore an environment while an invisible system collects behavioural data and gradually generates interpretations of the user.

The first stage of the project focused on identifying behavioural data that could be collected through simple interactions.

Unlike traditional datasets, these data sources are generated directly through user behaviour and continuously evolve during interaction.

## Initial Concept Development

Initially, I was interested in emotional visualisation and how emotions might be represented through interactive systems.

However, after reviewing my proposal and reflecting on the ideas explored in Data Humanism, I realised that the more interesting question was not how emotions are represented, but how systems construct interpretations from behavioural data.

This shifted the project from emotional representation towards identity construction and algorithmic interpretation.

## Consultation Reflection

During consultation, I discussed the possibility of creating a game-like interactive experience that responds to user behaviour.

The discussion helped clarify that the strongest aspect of the project was not the visual atmosphere itself, but the invisible process occurring behind it. Rather than creating a system that simply visualises emotion, I began focusing on how systems infer information about users through behavioural patterns.

This led me to consider ideas around algorithmic profiling, data interpretation, and identity construction.

As a result, I decided to structure the project around a progression from behaviour tracking to profile generation and reflection.

## Project Planning

I refined the overall structure of the project and established a development roadmap focused on iterative experimentation.

Rather than attempting to build the final experience immediately, I decided to divide the project into a series of prototypes that progressively explore data collection, interpretation, visualisation, and reflection.

## Next Steps

For Week 7, I plan to develop the first functional prototype.

The goal is to create a behaviour tracking system capable of recording movement, clicks, idle time, and session duration.

This prototype will establish the primary data source for the remainder of the project.

# Reflection

This week helped me move from a broad research direction towards a clearer project framework. While my original proposal focused on emotional visualisation, I realised that the most compelling aspect of the project lies in how systems interpret people through data. This shift significantly strengthened the conceptual foundation of the project and aligned more closely with ideas discussed in Data Humanism.

Through data exploration and precedent research, I began to see data as an active participant in shaping experiences rather than a neutral record of behaviour. This challenged my previous assumption that data visualisation should focus primarily on representation. Instead, I became interested in how data can influence perception, identity, and self-understanding.

Technically, this week also highlighted the importance of building the project incrementally. Rather than focusing on visual polish, I recognised that behaviour tracking and interpretation systems will form the conceptual core of the project. Establishing these systems early will allow future prototypes to develop from a strong foundation.

Moving forward, I aim to test how behavioural data can be collected and transformed into meaningful interpretations while maintaining a critical perspective on algorithmic profiling and identity construction.
